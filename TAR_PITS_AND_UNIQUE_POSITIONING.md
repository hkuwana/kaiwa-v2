- **Week 1 churn** â†’ Fix onboarding (get them to first value in <5 min)

### Framework: The "Unique Positioning Stack"

Most companies think they're unique, but they're not. Here's how to know if you're actually differentiated:

**Level 1: Feature Differentiation** (WEAK)
- "We have AI conversation practice!"
- Problem: Features are easy to copy (3-6 months)
- Example: ChatGPT could add scenarios tomorrow

**Level 2: Experience Differentiation** (MEDIUM)
- "Our product feels warm and empathetic, not gamified"
- Problem: Design/UX can be copied (6-12 months)
- Example: Duolingo could redesign with empathy theme

**Level 3: Positioning Differentiation** (STRONG)
- "We're for high-stakes conversations, not casual learning"
- Problem: Positioning can be copied but hard to execute
- Example: Duolingo could pivot to relationships, but brand inertia makes it hard

**Level 4: Community Differentiation** (VERY STRONG)
- "Our users practice together and celebrate real conversations"
- Problem: Community takes years to build, can't be copied quickly
- Example: Duolingo has forums, but not emotional community

**Level 5: Data Differentiation** (STRONGEST)
- "We have proprietary speech models trained on emotional conversations"
- Problem: Requires years of data collection, impossible to copy without users
- Example: ChatGPT can't replicate your dataset without your users

**Your goal**: Build differentiation at Levels 3-5, not 1-2.
